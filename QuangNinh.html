    Quang Ninh

    Quang Ninh is a coastal province in the Northeastern region of Vietnam. According to the economic development plan, Quang Ninh belongs to both the Northern Key Economic Zone and the Northern Coastal Region. This is the main coal mining province of Vietnam, with Ha Long Bay, a world heritage and natural wonder. Quang Ninh is also one of the eight provinces planned by the Vietnamese Government to become centrally-governed cities.

    • Airway system
    • Van Don International Airport is a civil-military airport located in Doan Ket Commune, Van Don District, Quang Ninh Province, Vietnam. This airport also functions as a new backup airport for Noi Bai. The airport is about 60 km from Ha Long City and nearly 20 km from Cam Pha City, mainly serving Van Don and Ha Long Special Zones with a capacity of up to 10 million passengers/year.
    • Operating airlines include: Vietnam Airlines, Bamboo Airways, Donghai Airlines, Qingdao Airlines, VietJet Air.
    • Railway
    • The railway is a railway connecting the capital Hanoi with the world's natural wonder Ha Long Bay, which is currently under construction.[1] The main railway is Vietnam's first high-speed railway. The route is designed with a passenger train speed of 120 km/h (the average speed of Vietnam's railways is currently 50 km/h), freight train speed of 80 km/h. /h .
    • Previously, to go to Ha Long, people had to take the train from Yen Vien station to Kep station (Hanoi - Dong Dang Railway) and then from Kep to Ha Long station (Kep - Cai Lan Railway), the new railway line. will reduce travel time from Hanoi to Ha Long.
    • waterway system
    • Waterway tourism becomes one of the types of tourism, tourism products that create a difference for the City, contributing to diversifying the City's tourism products. Strive for the total number of waterway transport vehicles serving tourists by 2025 to reach 200 canoes, 100 ships, boats and yachts of all kinds.
    • Quang Ninh Provincial Museum
      Tran Quoc Nghien Street, Ha Long, Quang Ninh
    • The new building of the Quang Ninh Museum is a modern architectural work designed by architect Salvador Pérez Arroyo in partnership with the National Institute of Urban and Rural Planning. It is a large box-shaped block, the exterior is covered by black tempered glass, the architect was inspired by coal, a main industrial product of Quang Ninh province. The museum is one of three blocks in the Complex, the remaining two blocks are the Library House and the Exhibition Workshop House.
    • Bai Chay Bridge
      Located on Highway 18, connecting two parts of Ha Long city, Hon Gai and Bai Chay, through Cua Luc Bay, where it flows into Ha Long Bay, in Quang Ninh province.
    • The purpose of this bridge solves the travel needs of Quang Ninh people, domestic and foreign tourists, and ends the decades-long operation of the Bai Chay ferry. Not only meaningful in terms of traffic, Bai Chay Bridge marks new construction records in Vietnam and the world. With slim, modern architecture, Bai Chay Bridge brings a new and romantic architectural feature, contributing to the beauty of Ha Long Bay.
    • Quang Ninh Provincial Gate
      Ha Long - Noi Bai highway , Binh Duong, Dong Trieu, Quang Ninh
    • The architecture of the gate to welcome Quang Ninh province is a combination of two elements of the world natural heritage of Ha Long Bay and Yen Tu Buddhist Center including lotus towers, ornamental lakes, artificial hills, and landscape traffic.
    • Ha Long Bay
      Quang Ninh, Viet Nam
      . The UNESCO World Heritage Site covers an area of 434 km², including 775 islands, of which 411 have been named. This is home to many famous rocky islands, caves and beaches.
    • Tuan Chau Peninsula
      Ha Long city, Quang Ninh, Vietnam.
      Possessing peaceful beauty, open air and being invested in the construction of an amusement park, the largest and most modern cruise ship port, Tuan Chau Quang Ninh island has become the most attractive destination in the coastal city of Ha Long.
